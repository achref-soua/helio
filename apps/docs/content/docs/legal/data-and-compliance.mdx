## Where your data lives

Everything a Helio deployment processes stays in infrastructure the operator
runs:

| Store                  | What it holds                                                            |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| PostgreSQL             | Contacts, companies, segments, journeys, templates, configuration, audit |
| ClickHouse             | Behavioral events, opens, clicks (full profile)                          |
| Redis                  | Cache, sessions, rate limits                                             |
| Your object storage    | Assets and exports                                                       |
| Your backups directory | Database dumps you schedule and control                                  |

No copy of any of it reaches the Helio project. Data leaves your deployment
only through providers **you** connect (email relay, SMS, WhatsApp, AI) and
through messages you choose to send.

## Security architecture

The [Enterprise & security](/docs/security/permissions) section covers each
in depth; the compliance-relevant spine:

- **Tenant isolation enforced by the database**: every tenant table carries
  PostgreSQL row-level security, so cross-organization reads are impossible
  even for buggy queries.
- **Secrets sealed at rest**: provider credentials, DKIM keys, and signing
  secrets are encrypted with AES-256-GCM under a deployment key
  ([credential vault](/docs/security/credential-vault)), bound to their row,
  never logged, with zero-downtime rotation.
- **Account security**: TOTP two-factor (org-enforceable), password policy
  with forced rotation, OIDC SSO, SCIM provisioning, scoped API keys.
- **Accountability**: an append-only audit log of security-relevant actions;
  outbound webhooks are HMAC-signed; public endpoints are rate-limited.
- **Transport**: AI prompts are refused over plaintext HTTP to remote hosts;
  put TLS in front of every public surface (see
  [self-hosting](/docs/self-hosting)).

## GDPR tooling for operators

Helio ships the mechanics; the legal obligations are yours as controller:

- **Data-subject export** — a contact's row menu produces a JSON export of
  everything held about them ([contacts guide](/docs/guides/contacts)).
- **Erasure** — deleting a contact removes the profile and cascades through
  the tenant's data.
- **Consent and suppression** — unsubscribes, bounces, and complaints land
  on a suppression list that every send path honors, automatically.
- **Minimization by architecture** — there is no third-party processor in a
  default install; each one you add is an explicit, auditable choice.

## Email & messaging compliance

- **One-click unsubscribe** (RFC 8058) header and link on every marketing
  email, plus a hosted preference center — the CAN-SPAM/GDPR baseline.
- **Transactional and marketing streams are separate**, so receipts and
  resets never carry marketing obligations.
- **Deliverability hygiene** — the [deliverability wizard](/docs/guides/deliverability)
  sets up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC per sending domain; authenticated mail is
  both a compliance and an inbox-placement requirement.
- Quiet hours and frequency caps in journeys help honor jurisdictional
  contact-time rules.

## AI and data sovereignty

Bring your own provider — including a fully local OpenAI-compatible server —
and the copilot's context never leaves your network. Keys are sealed in the
credential vault; each organization can use its own
([AI copilot guide](/docs/guides/ai-copilot)).

## Operator checklist

1. Publish a privacy policy for your audience; you are the controller.
2. Sign DPAs with the providers you connect (email relay, SMS, AI).
3. Put TLS in front of the stack and set strong `HELIO_ENCRYPTION_KEY`
   custody.
4. Schedule [backups](/docs/guides/backup-restore) and rehearse a restore.
5. Decide retention: events and exports accumulate until you prune them.
6. Use double opt-in where your jurisdictions expect it, and import only
   lists with provenance you can defend.

<Callout type="info">
  Compliance is a property of an operation, not of software alone. Helio gives you the controls;
  using them correctly for your jurisdictions is a conversation to have with your counsel.
</Callout>
