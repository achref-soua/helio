title: Meeting scheduler
description: Share a booking link; invitees pick an open slot and it lands as a CRM meeting.
---

The scheduler turns your availability into a public booking page. Share the
link, and invitees pick an open time — the meeting is created, the invitee
becomes a contact, and a follow-up task lands in your CRM.

## The booking page

Configure it under **Scheduling**: a meeting name, duration, a buffer between
meetings, a timezone, and the weekdays and hours you're available. Save it to
get a public link at `/m/<id>` — share it anywhere.

Availability is expressed in the page's timezone. Open slots are computed for
the next two weeks: each availability window is laid out into duration-sized
slots (stepped by duration + buffer), with past and already-booked times
removed.

## Booking

The public page lists open slots in the page's timezone. When an invitee picks
one and enters their email:

- the slot is **re-validated on the server** — an invitee can't book the past,
  an off-hours time, or a slot that was just taken;
- a **meeting** is created (the unique `(page, start)` makes a double-book a
  caught conflict, not a duplicate);
- the invitee is **upserted as a contact** in the workspace (existing contacts
  are never downgraded or resurrected from unsubscribed);
- a **MEETING task** is filed for the page owner, linked to the contact, so the
  meeting shows up next to your [deals and tasks](/docs/guides/crm).

Booking pages are [white-labeled](/docs/guides/white-labeling) with your org's
branding, like hosted forms. Cancel a meeting from the **Upcoming meetings**
list on the Scheduling page.
