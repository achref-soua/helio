title: Campaigns
description: One-off sends to a segment or list, delivered durably on Temporal, with subject and content A/B testing.

A campaign is a one-off send — a newsletter, a launch announcement, a re-engagement
blast — to a [segment](/docs/guides/segments) or [list](/docs/guides/contacts).

## How a send works

When you launch a campaign, Helio:

1. Enumerates the audience, **excluding suppressed contacts** at enumeration
   time.
2. Creates one **send row per recipient**. This row is what makes delivery
   idempotent: a retry after a crash re-reads the row and never sends twice.
3. Runs the send on **Temporal**, so a worker restart mid-campaign resumes
   cleanly rather than double-sending or dropping recipients.
4. Re-checks suppression **per message** before each send, in case a contact
   unsubscribed between enumeration and delivery.

## A/B testing

Test a **subject line** or **content** variant. Helio assigns each recipient a
sticky variant (the same contact always gets the same variant), records the
variant on the send row and in every engagement event, and reports per-variant
opens and clicks so you can read significance.

### Autonomous winner selection

Turn on **auto-winner** and Helio runs a holdout test phase, evaluates the
variants with a two-proportion z-test once the sample window closes, promotes
the winning variant to the remaining audience, and badges the winner in the UI
— no manual checking required. See the
[AI copilot guide](/docs/guides/ai-copilot) for the broader set of automated
optimizations.

## Send-time optimization

When enabled, Helio can defer each recipient's send to the hour they have
historically been most likely to open or click, derived from their own
engagement history.

## Analytics

Every campaign reports opens, clicks, and conversions into
[analytics](/docs/guides/analytics), with per-variant breakdowns for A/B tests.
