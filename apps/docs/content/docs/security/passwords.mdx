## Forgot / reset

**Forgot password?** on the login page emails a reset link (through your
organization's own email provider, or the deployment SMTP). The reply is
identical whether or not the address exists — a different answer would
confirm accounts to strangers. Reset links are single-use and expire.

## Strength, not theater

Signup, first-run setup, and every password change show a live strength
meter (zxcvbn — the estimator that thinks in guesses, not character
classes). Weak choices can't be submitted; the server keeps its own
minimum regardless of what a client claims.

## Active sessions

**Settings → Security** lists every signed-in device with its browser and
IP. Sign out any single session, or everything except the one you're on.
Changing your password signs out all other sessions automatically.

## Rotation policy (admins)

**Settings → Password policy** lets an admin require a fresh password
every _N_ days (7–365, default 90), per organization:

- Members past the deadline are routed to a **forced change screen** at
  sign-in — the only ways forward are a new password or signing out.
- The clock starts at each password change (tracked server-side on every
  change, reset, or admin reset).
- **SSO-only members are unaffected** — they have no Helio password.
- Policy changes are audit-logged.

<Callout type="info">
  Rotation policies are an organizational compliance tool. If you don't have a compliance reason,
  long unique passwords + two-factor beat forced rotation — which is why the policy ships off.
</Callout>
