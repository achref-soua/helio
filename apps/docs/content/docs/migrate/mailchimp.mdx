---
title: From Mailchimp
description: Export a Mailchimp audience and import it into Helio, keeping unsubscribed and cleaned contacts suppressed.
---

Helio detects Mailchimp audience exports and maps Mailchimp's subscription
status to Helio's suppression list.

## Export from Mailchimp

1. In Mailchimp, go to **Audience → All contacts**.
2. Use **Export Audience** to download a CSV. Mailchimp includes the
   subscription **status** column (subscribed, unsubscribed, cleaned,
   non-subscribed).
3. If you keep multiple audiences, export each one separately.

## Import into Helio

1. Open **Contacts → Import** in Helio and drop the CSV.
2. Helio detects the Mailchimp format and shows the count of contacts that will
   be imported as **unsubscribed**.
3. Review the validation summary and confirm.

## What carries over

- **Email** and name fields map to the built-in fields.
- Contacts marked **unsubscribed**, **cleaned**, or otherwise non-mailable
  import as `UNSUBSCRIBED` and join the
  [suppression list](/docs/guides/contacts#suppression--privacy), so they're
  never accidentally emailed.
- Merge fields and tags become [attributes](/docs/guides/contacts).

<Callout type="warn">
  Mailchimp **cleaned** contacts (hard-bounced or flagged) are treated as suppressed on purpose —
  re-mailing them would hurt your deliverability.
</Callout>

Next, recreate your audiences as [segments](/docs/guides/segments) — because
segments are live predicates, they stay current without a sync step.
